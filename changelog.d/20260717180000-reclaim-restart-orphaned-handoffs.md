Reclaim background-job handoffs orphaned by a main restart. A fresh `background-jobs-main` holds no in-memory leases, so every pre-existing `handed_off` row was orphaned from its perspective and previously sat — holding its durable concurrency slot — until the hours-long age-based orphan sweep, which after every deploy could choke capped queues. The main now waits a short startup grace period for workers to reconnect and re-announce their ids, then reclaims exactly the handoffs whose worker did not come back (returning them to the queue and releasing their reservations). A worker that survives the main restart keeps its id and reconnects, so its in-flight jobs are preserved; only genuinely orphaned handoffs are reclaimed. Grace period overridable via `startupHandoffReclaimGraceMs` (default 15000ms).
