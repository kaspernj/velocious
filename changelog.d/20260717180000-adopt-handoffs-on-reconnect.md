Adopt a worker's in-flight handoffs when it reconnects to a restarted main. A fresh `background-jobs-main` (e.g. after a deploy) holds no in-memory leases, so a worker that reconnects with its stable id previously had its pre-restart jobs tracked nowhere — if it then died, those leases and their durable concurrency reservations sat stuck until the hours-long orphan sweep. The main now adopts a reconnecting worker's still-active `handed_off` jobs into its socket's lease map on `hello`, so a later disconnect releases them normally. It does not time-reclaim handoffs of workers that have not reconnected: a gracefully-draining worker from the old release keeps running (and reporting) its jobs without reconnecting its control socket, so a timer-based requeue would double-run it; genuinely orphaned handoffs (a crash) are left to the age-based sweep.
