The long-lived background job worker no longer drops a job's terminal status when persisting it fails transiently. When a worker reports a completion/failure and jobs-main's `store.markCompleted`/`markFailed` throws (a deadlock, connection reset, lock-wait timeout, or main's cold connection pool right after a deploy restart), main answers `job-update-error`. `BackgroundJobsStatusReporter#reportWithRetry` threw that immediately and the worker logged and dropped it, permanently stranding the job in `handed_off` — and for a `max_concurrency: 1` job such as a build/queue planner, that single stranded row blocked every future run until an operator intervened. `reportWithRetry` now takes an opt-in `retryPersistErrors` flag: the worker (which is long-lived and cannot exit to trigger orphan reclaim) sets it and retries the transient persist failure with backoff until the status lands, while short-lived forked/spawned runners keep the existing behavior of throwing immediately so they exit non-zero and are reclaimed. `job-update-error` is only ever answered when the DB write throws (every logical rejection answers `job-updated`), so it is always the transient, retryable kind.
