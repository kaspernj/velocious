Background-jobs workers now drain and reap their forked `background-jobs-runner` children on graceful stop instead of orphaning them across a deploy. The drain window is configurable with `VELOCIOUS_BACKGROUND_JOBS_WORKER_SHUTDOWN_TIMEOUT_MS` (default `indefinite`: wait for in-flight jobs and never interrupt a running job; a positive millisecond value finishes in-flight jobs up to that long and then terminates any forked runners still in flight). See `docs/background-jobs.md`.
