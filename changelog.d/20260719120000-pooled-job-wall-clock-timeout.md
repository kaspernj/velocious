Extended the background-job `jobTimeoutMs` wall-clock backstop to pooled jobs. A genuinely-hung pooled job previously pinned its runner's concurrency slot indefinitely (the lifetime recycle only retires a child once its in-flight set drains, which a hung job never does). On overrun the worker now terminates the child running it (SIGTERM, then SIGKILL after the grace); its in-flight jobs are reported failed and requeued, and a replacement child is spawned.
