## Fixed

- The websocket event-log store and the background-jobs store now re-verify that their lazily-created internal tables physically exist before trusting their cached "ready" state, recreating the schema when it is missing. Previously a per-configuration store would create its tables (`websocket_channel_events`/`websocket_replay_channels`, or `background_jobs`) inside another caller's database transaction; when that transaction rolled back, the DDL was reverted (SQLite/MSSQL DDL is transactional) but the in-memory ready flag / migration row stayed set, so every later caller skipped re-creating the table and failed with `SQLITE_ERROR: no such table` (or the MSSQL equivalent). This surfaced under transaction-based test cleaning when a transaction-cleaned spec was the first to touch a store.
